How to Carve a Pumpkin

By Tim Van Driel

Materials | Step 1 | Step 2 | Step 3 | Step 4

What you'll need:

  • A pumpkin
  • A carving knife
  • A spoon
  • A candle
  • A lighter
  • A sharpie

Turner Farms

Turner Farms is a great place to get pumpkins. They have a wide variety of pumpkins to choose from. They also have a corn maze and a petting zoo. It's a great place to go with your family. A map is located below.

Step 1: Cut a hole in the top of your pumpkin around the stem

The hole does not need to be perfect, but allows for you to reach the guts inside the pumpkin. Once you have cut the hole, remove the stem and set it aside. You will need it later. Next:

  • Remove the seeds and guts from the pumpkin using your spoon.
  • Optional: If you wish to make roasted pumpkin seeds place the seeds in a bowl and set aside. Click here for pumpkin seed recipe. Otherwise you may throw them away.
  • Once you have removed all the seeds and guts move onto the next step.

Step 2: Draw your design on the pumpkin

Using your sharpie, draw your design on the pumpkin. This can be anything you want. Once you have drawn your design, move onto the next step. Tip: If you are having trouble drawing your design, you can print out a stencil and tape it to the pumpkin. Then you can trace the stencil onto the pumpkin. Click here for some free stencils.

Step 3: Carve your design into the pumpkin

Using your carving knife, carve your design into the pumpkin. Be careful not to cut yourself. Once you have finished carving your design, move onto the next step.

Step 4: Place a candle inside the pumpkin

Place a candle inside the pumpkin and light it. Then place the stem back on top of the pumpkin. You now have a carved pumpkin. Congratulations!
